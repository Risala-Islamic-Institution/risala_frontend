CHAPTER ONE

1. Introduction

1.1 Background of the Organization / Background of the Project

In recent years, the rapid advancement of information and communication technologies has
significantly transformed the way knowledge is accessed, shared, and delivered across the world.
Digital platforms have become central to education, enabling learners to overcome geographical,
time, and resource constraints. This transformation has particularly impacted professional and
adult learners who seek flexible learning opportunities that align with their daily responsibilities.
As a result, online and technology-assisted learning systems have become essential tools for
bridging gaps between knowledge providers and learners in various fields.

Despite the global growth of digital education platforms, access to structured and flexible Islamic
education remains limited, especially for adult learners who did not have the opportunity to learn
foundational religious knowledge earlier in life. In many Muslim communities, including
Ethiopia and other developing regions, Quranic and Islamic learning has traditionally been
delivered through physical madrasas, mosques, or informal study circles. While these institutions
play a vital role, their rigid schedules, location dependency, and limited instructor availability
often make them inaccessible to working adults, parents, and individuals with demanding daily
routines.

At the same time, there exists a growing number of qualified Islamic teachers (Ustaz) who
possess strong knowledge in Quran recitation, Tajwid, Hadith, Arabic language, and other
Islamic disciplines, but lack access to modern platforms that allow them to offer their services
beyond their immediate local communities. Many of these teachers rely solely on in-person
teaching methods, which restrict their reach and limit their ability to serve learners who are
willing and able to learn but cannot physically attend traditional learning centers. This disconnect
between learners and teachers creates an inefficiency in the delivery of Islamic knowledge in the
modern digital era.

The emergence of online learning marketplaces in other domains, such as language learning and
professional skill development, demonstrates the effectiveness of platforms that connect learners
directly with instructors through flexible scheduling and transparent pricing models. However,
existing global platforms rarely address the unique needs of Islamic education, particularly with
respect to cultural sensitivity, trust, structured religious guidance, and teacher verification.
Furthermore, most existing Islamic learning platforms focus narrowly on Quran recitation alone,
often neglecting broader Islamic knowledge areas such as Hadith studies, Arabic language
learning, and Islamic skills like calligraphy and religious arts.

Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills PLC is conceived in response to these challenges. The
organization aims to create a digital learning platform that connects learners with verified
Islamic knowledge providers through a professional, flexible, and scalable system. The platform
is designed as a marketplace where Islamic teachers independently define their availability,
session duration, and pricing, while learners are empowered to choose instructors that best match
their time schedules, learning goals, and financial capacity. Unlike traditional online madrasas,
Risala does not employ or own the teachers; instead, it facilitates a trusted digital environment
that enables ethical, transparent, and mutually beneficial interactions between learners and
educators.

The background of this project is rooted in the recognition that modern Muslim learners--
especially working adults--require learning systems that adapt to their lives rather than forcing
them to adapt to rigid institutional structures. By leveraging modern web technologies, secure
payment systems, and real-time scheduling mechanisms, Risala seeks to modernize the delivery
of Islamic knowledge while preserving its authenticity, integrity, and purpose. The project is
initiated as a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) focusing on working adult Quran beginners, with
a long-term vision of expanding into a comprehensive digital ecosystem for Islamic knowledge
and skills.

Ultimately, this project represents an intersection between technology and religious education,
aiming to address long-standing accessibility challenges while opening new opportunities for
both learners and teachers. Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills PLC positions itself not merely
as an online learning platform, but as a sustainable digital infrastructure that supports lifelong
Islamic learning in a rapidly evolving digital world.

1.2 Statement of the Problem

Access to structured and flexible Islamic education, particularly Quran learning for adults,
remains a significant challenge in many parts of the world. While the desire to learn the Quran
exists among working adults, professionals, and late beginners, existing learning methods are
often rigid, location-bound, and not designed to accommodate modern lifestyles. Traditional
Quran learning centers usually require physical presence at fixed times, which makes them
inaccessible for individuals with full-time jobs, family responsibilities, or irregular schedules.

Another major problem is the lack of learner-centered flexibility in current online Islamic
learning platforms. Many existing systems rely on fixed class schedules determined by
institutions rather than by individual teachers (Ustaz). This one-size-fits-all scheduling approach
does not reflect the real availability of qualified teachers, nor does it allow students to choose
learning times that match their personal routines. As a result, learners are either forced to
compromise on timing or abandon learning altogether.

There is also a significant issue related to transparency and efficiency in teacher availability. In
many platforms, students cannot clearly see when a teacher is available, which time slots are
already booked, or how schedules change dynamically. This often leads to double bookings,
manual coordination through messaging applications, delayed responses, and confusion between
students and teachers. Such inefficiencies reduce trust in the platform and negatively impact the
learning experience.

Furthermore, working adult Quran beginners often struggle to find teachers who understand their
specific learning needs. Most existing platforms focus either on children or on full-time students,
leaving adult beginners underserved. These learners require a supportive environment, flexible
pacing, and skills-oriented instruction that balances Quran recitation, basic tajwid, and practical
understanding, all within limited time slots.

From the teachers' perspective, many qualified Ustaz lack a professional digital platform that
allows them to independently manage their teaching schedules, showcase their availability, and
connect with students globally. Most rely on informal arrangements through social media or
messaging apps, which do not scale well and offer no systematic way to manage time, payments,
or student progress.

In addition, there is a gap in integrating both knowledge and practical skills within a single
Islamic learning platform. Existing solutions often focus solely on content delivery without
emphasizing structured skill development, such as gradual Quran recitation improvement,
consistent practice schedules, and measurable learning outcomes.

The absence of a centralized, well-structured, and technology-driven system that connects
working adult learners with qualified teachers--while allowing teachers to control their own
schedules--creates a critical gap in modern Islamic education. This problem highlights the need
for a flexible, transparent, and skill-oriented digital platform that supports dynamic scheduling,
real-time availability management, and learner-focused Quran education.

Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills PLC is designed to address these challenges by providing
a professional digital platform where Ustaz can define their own recurring availability (for
example, specific days and time ranges), and students can view real-time schedules, select
available time slots, and book sessions without conflict. By combining flexible scheduling,
transparency, and a focus on both Islamic knowledge and practical learning skills, the proposed
system aims to overcome the limitations of existing Quran learning methods and platforms.

1.4.2 System Development Process Model

A system development process model defines the structured steps followed to design, develop,
test, and deploy a software system. These models help manage complexity, reduce development
risks, and ensure that the final product meets user requirements.

For this project, the Agile Software Development Model is selected. Agile emphasizes iterative
development, continuous feedback, and incremental delivery of system features. Unlike
traditional linear models, Agile allows flexibility in accommodating changing requirements,
which is essential for systems involving multiple user roles such as students, teachers, and
administrators.

The Agile model is suitable for this project because:

     User requirements, especially related to scheduling and learning preferences, may evolve
         during development.

     Continuous feedback from Ustaz and students is necessary to refine system features.
     The system can be developed in small functional modules such as authentication,

         scheduling, booking, and learning management.
     Early testing and validation reduce the risk of major failures at later stages.

The Agile process followed in this project includes requirement gathering, iterative design,
implementation, testing, review, and refinement. A diagram illustrating the Agile development
cycle is included in this section to visually represent the development flow.

1.4.3 Design Pattern

A design pattern provides a reusable solution to common software design problems and
improves system maintainability, scalability, and clarity.

This project follows the Model�View�Controller (MVC) design pattern. The MVC pattern
separates the system into three interconnected components: the Model, the View, and the
Controller.

     The Model represents the system's data and business logic, such as users, schedules,
         bookings, and learning content.

     The View handles the user interface and presentation layer, displaying information to
         users in a clear and accessible manner.

     The Controller acts as an intermediary between the Model and the View, processing user
         requests, applying business rules, and returning appropriate responses.

The MVC pattern is chosen because it:
     Promotes separation of concerns, making the system easier to understand and maintain.
     Supports parallel development of frontend and backend components.
     Is well-supported by modern web frameworks used in this project.
     Enhances scalability and code reusability.

By applying MVC, the system achieves a clean architecture that simplifies future enhancements
and maintenance.

1.9 Risk Assessment

Risk assessment is a critical component of project planning, aimed at identifying, analyzing, and
mitigating potential risks that could affect the development, deployment, and operation of the
Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills platform. This section outlines the major risks associated
with the project and strategies to minimize their impact, ensuring project success and
sustainability.

1.9.1 Risk

The following risks have been identified as potentially impacting the project:

    1. Technical Risks
             o Risk: Integration challenges between backend (Django REST Framework) and
                  frontend (Next.js + Tailwind CSS) could delay development.
                  Mitigation: Follow modular architecture, conduct iterative testing, and ensure
                  clear API documentation.
             o Risk: Scalability issues may arise if user traffic increases rapidly.
                  Mitigation: Utilize cloud-based deployment with scalable server instances and
                  load balancing to handle high concurrent usage.
             o Risk: Video conferencing integration may face latency or compatibility issues.
                  Mitigation: Use tested APIs from reliable providers, perform extensive cross-
                  device testing, and provide fallback solutions.

    2. Operational Risks
             o Risk: Teachers (Ustaz/Ustaza) may have inconsistent scheduling or fail to update
                  availability.
                  Mitigation: Implement automated reminders and validation checks to ensure
                  schedules are accurately maintained.
             o Risk: Students may double-book sessions due to system errors.
                  Mitigation: Implement real-time booking validation and locking of time slots
                  upon confirmation.

    3. Financial Risks
             o Risk: Low initial adoption could impact revenue projections and sustainability.
                  Mitigation: Launch targeted marketing campaigns and referral programs to
                  attract early users.
             o Risk: Payment gateway failures or transaction delays could affect trust and
                  revenue.
                  Mitigation: Use multiple secure payment providers and implement transaction
                  monitoring and alerting.

    4. Legal and Compliance Risks
             o Risk: Non-compliance with local or international data protection regulations.
                  Mitigation: Implement strong encryption, secure data storage, and adhere to
                  privacy policies and legal guidelines.
             o Risk: Potential copyright or content licensing issues for learning materials.
                  Mitigation: Use verified, original, or properly licensed content only.

    5. Social and Cultural Risks
             o Risk: Misinterpretation or misuse of Islamic content leading to negative
                  feedback.
                  Mitigation: Ensure content review by qualified scholars, provide disclaimers, and
                  maintain clear content moderation policies.

CHAPTER TWO

2. Business Area Analysis and Requirement Definition
2.1 Introduction

Business area analysis is a critical phase in system development that focuses on understanding
the operational environment, stakeholders, workflows, and challenges within the domain where
the proposed system will operate. This analysis ensures that the system requirements are aligned
with real-world needs and organizational objectives.

The Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills platform operates within the digital education and
religious learning domain. The platform is designed to bridge the gap between learners seeking
Islamic knowledge and qualified instructors offering various Islamic disciplines and skills. These
include Qur'an learning, Tajwid improvement, Hadith studies, Arabic language instruction, and
Islamic creative skills such as calligraphy and related arts.

This chapter presents a detailed analysis of the current business environment, identifies existing
challenges, defines the rules governing the system, and introduces the proposed system. It also
establishes clear system requirements that will guide the design and implementation phases. The
analysis is conducted to ensure the system is user-centered, scalable, and aligned with both
educational and organizational goals.

2.2 Current System

The current system for acquiring Islamic knowledge and skills primarily relies on traditional,
informal, and semi-digital methods. In many communities, learning is conducted through
physical mosques, madrassas, private tutors, or informal study groups. While these methods have
served learners for generations, they face significant limitations in accessibility, flexibility,
scalability, and organization.

In some cases, learners depend on personal referrals to find instructors, which limits choice and
transparency. Scheduling is often manual and inconsistent, making it difficult for working adults
and learners with busy schedules to participate regularly. Payment processes are typically
informal, lacking standardized pricing or clear service structures.

Although some global online platforms exist, they often focus on limited subject areas, do not
fully accommodate Islamic learning ethics, or lack customization for local contexts such as
language, time zones, and cultural expectations. Additionally, many instructors are unfamiliar
with digital teaching tools and lack platforms that support them professionally.

Overall, the current system lacks a centralized, structured, and user-friendly digital solution
that integrates instructor discovery, scheduling, payment, and live teaching in a single platform
while supporting diverse Islamic knowledge and skills.

2.2.1 Players of the Existing System

The existing system involves multiple stakeholders who interact through fragmented and often
inefficient processes. The primary players include:

Learners (Students)

Learners consist of individuals from different age groups and backgrounds seeking Islamic
knowledge or skills. This includes working adults, beginners in Qur'an reading, students
improving Tajwid, language learners, and individuals interested in Islamic arts. Learners often
face challenges in finding suitable instructors, flexible schedules, and structured learning paths.

Instructors (Ustaz/Ustaza)

Instructors are individuals with expertise in Islamic knowledge or skills. They may teach in
mosques, private settings, or informal online sessions. Many instructors rely on word-of-mouth
to find students and lack professional digital tools to manage schedules, payments, and learner
communication.

Religious Institutions
Mosques, madrassas, and Islamic centers act as learning hubs but are limited by physical
capacity, geographic reach, and fixed schedules. Their offerings may not align with the
availability of modern learners, especially working adults.

Informal Online Tools

Tools such as messaging apps and video conferencing platforms are sometimes used to facilitate
learning. However, these tools operate independently and lack integrated scheduling, payment
handling, learner management, and quality assurance mechanisms.

2.3 Business Rules

Business rules define the constraints, policies, and conditions under which the Risala Islamic
Knowledge and Skills platform operates. These rules ensure proper system behavior, role-based
access control, data integrity, and consistent interaction among learners, instructors, and
administrators.

The following business rules govern the operation of the proposed system:

     BR-01: All instructors (Ustaz/Ustaza) must provide accurate personal information,
         qualifications, and areas of expertise during registration before being approved to offer
         services on the platform.

     BR-02: Instructor accounts must be reviewed and approved by the system administrator
         before they become visible to learners.

     BR-03: Learners must register and authenticate through the system before booking any

     BR-04: Instructors are responsible for defining their own availability schedules within

     BR-05: Only time slots explicitly activated by an instructor shall be displayed as

     BR-06: Once a learner successfully books a session, the selected time slot must be

     BR-07: Learners can only book sessions that fall within the instructor's published

     BR-08: Instructors are not permitted to modify or cancel confirmed sessions without
         following the system's cancellation and rescheduling policies.

     BR-09: The system must automatically generate and assign live session access details

     BR-10: Learners can only access their own profiles, bookings, and session history; access

     BR-11: Instructors can only access their own profiles, schedules, session records, and

2.4 Proposed System

The proposed system is a web-based digital learning marketplace designed to connect learners
seeking Islamic knowledge and skills with qualified instructors through a centralized, secure, and
user-friendly platform. The system aims to modernize the process of Islamic education delivery
by integrating instructor discovery, scheduling, payment processing, and live session
management into a single unified solution.

Unlike traditional learning methods and fragmented digital tools, the proposed system introduces
a structured and scalable approach that supports multiple Islamic disciplines and skills. These
include Qur'an learning, Tajwid improvement, Hadith studies, Arabic language instruction, and
Islamic creative skills such as calligraphy and related arts. The system is designed to serve
diverse user groups, including working adults, beginners, advanced learners, and instructors with
varying levels of digital experience.

The proposed system addresses the limitations of the current system by offering flexibility,
transparency, and automation. Learners can browse instructors based on subject, availability,
pricing, and expertise, while instructors can independently manage their teaching schedules and
services. Administrative functions ensure quality control, role management, and platform
governance.

2.4.1 Overview

The Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills platform operates as a role-based system with three
primary user categories: learners (students), instructors (Ustaz/Ustaza), and system
administrators. Each role interacts with the system through controlled access rights and clearly
defined functionalities.

From the learner's perspective, the system provides an intuitive interface for discovering
instructors, viewing real-time availability schedules, booking live sessions, and accessing session
details. Learners are only shown time slots that are actively published and not already booked,
ensuring clarity and preventing scheduling conflicts.

From the instructor's perspective, the system enables instructors to define their teaching services,
set availability ranges (such as recurring weekly schedules), determine session durations within
allowed limits, and manage bookings. Once an instructor activates a schedule, it becomes visible
to learners, and any booked slot is automatically marked as unavailable.

From the administrative perspective, the system supports instructor verification, category
management, commission configuration, dispute handling, and platform monitoring.
Administrators ensure compliance with platform policies, ethical guidelines, and operational
standards.

The system is implemented as a web-based application using modern technologies, with a
frontend built using Next.js and Tailwind CSS, and a backend developed using Django and
Django REST Framework. Communication between frontend and backend is handled through
secure RESTful APIs. The platform integrates third-party services for video conferencing and
payment processing, ensuring reliable live sessions and secure financial transactions.

Overall, the proposed system provides a flexible, scalable, and sustainable digital infrastructure
that supports the growth of Islamic knowledge dissemination while maintaining professionalism,
trust, and accessibility.

2.4.3.2 Sequence Diagram

2.4.3.2.1 Overview

A sequence diagram illustrates how different actors and system components interact with each
other over time to complete a specific task.
In the proposed Risala Islamic Knowledge and Skills Platform, sequence diagrams are used to
model dynamic interactions such as user authentication, session booking, payment processing,
and live session initiation.

This section presents the primary and most critical sequence in the system:
 Booking and Conducting a Live Learning Session

This sequence represents the core business value of the platform.

2.4.3.2.2 Sequence Diagram Description: Book and Attend Live Session

Actors and Components Involved

     Learner (Student)
     Instructor (Ustaz)
     Web Application (Frontend � Next.js)
     Backend API (Django REST Framework)
     Database (Supabase / PostgreSQL)
     Payment Gateway
     Video Conferencing Service

Textual Flow of the Sequence (Narrative Explanation)

    1. The Learner logs into the system using valid credentials.
    2. The system authenticates the Learner and grants access to the learner dashboard.
    3. The Learner browses available instructors and applies filters such as subject, price, and

         available time.
    4. The system retrieves instructor profiles and availability schedules from the database.
    5. The Learner selects a preferred instructor and views the instructor's available time slots.
    6. The Learner selects a desired time slot and submits a booking request.
    7. The system checks the availability of the selected time slot to prevent conflicts.
    8. The system redirects the Learner to the payment process.
    9. The Learner completes the payment through the integrated payment gateway.
    10. The payment gateway validates the transaction and sends the payment status to the

         system.
    11. Upon successful payment, the system confirms the booking and marks the selected time

         slot as unavailable.
    12. The system generates a live session link using the video conferencing service.
    13. Notifications are sent to both the Learner and the Instructor.
    14. At the scheduled time, the Learner and Instructor join the live session using the generated
         link.

    15. After the session ends, the system updates session status and records attendance.
    16. The Learner is allowed to submit feedback and rating for the Instructor.

Why This Sequence Diagram Is Correct and Safe

 Matches teacher-defined schedules
 Prevents double booking
 Separates frontend, backend, and external services
 Reflects real-world payment and live session flow
 Aligns perfectly with previously defined use cases

     System design validation
     Implementation phase
     Defense / project presentation
